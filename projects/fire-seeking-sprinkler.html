Fire-Seeking Sprinkler

A targeted sprinkler concept designed to direct water toward the detected flame source, reducing unnecessary water exposure during residential fires.

ProjectSkillsUSA Engineering Technology Design
RoleTeam Leader
FocusFire detection, prototyping, 3D modeling

Work details

Understanding the challenge

Residential sprinklers are effective at suppressing flames, but broad activation can lead to significant water damage. The project explored whether a sprinkler head could respond more selectively by detecting the direction of a flame and releasing water only through the relevant outlet.

Designing a practical solution

The team interviewed off-duty firefighters in Kitsap County, sketched concepts, and modeled components that could detect heat directionally. The prototype used an internal wax-seal concept, radial conduits, infrared sensors, and ignitor-triggered melting to open the correct water path.

Design solution

The final mid-fidelity prototype placed second at the state competition. While it did not advance beyond that stage, the feedback clarified next steps: a more durable mechanical body, more accurate detection, and additional testing around reliability and safe activation.
