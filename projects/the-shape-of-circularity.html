The Shape of Circularity

Designing a ReUse Commons for Seattle REconomy: a spatial design, research, and 3D visualization project that translates a 9,000+ RSF expansion into a shared resource hub for reuse, repair, education, and material redistribution.

SponsorSeattle REconomy / Shoreline Tool Library
RoleResearch, spatial design, Blender visualization, Photo/Video Coordinator
TeamJane Pietsch, Theo Tzankov, Jack Strickland, Olivia Stovall

Project overview

Background

Seattle REconomy is a nonprofit focused on promoting a circular economy through reuse, repair, and sharing. With a city grant securing more than 9,000 square feet of new space, our goal was to design an optimal layout for a ReUse Commons: a multi-sector community resource hub beyond a tool library.

Design question

How might we design a shared, community-scale space that enables Seattle REconomy's programs, partners, and community members to collaborate effectively while supporting reuse, repair, and material redistribution?

Pain points

Staff and volunteers were stretched by high demand, donations, and clutter. As outside stakeholders, our team helped prioritize goals, navigate dependencies, and envision the Shoreline space as a true ReUse Commons that could support each sector's needs.

Process

  • Research synthesis through literature review, field observation, and six stakeholder interviews.
  • Low-fidelity layout work using journey mapping and two co-design workshops.
  • Physical usability testing with a 3D printed blueprint and two interactive testing sessions.
  • Digital rendering in Blender to communicate the full spatial proposal to partners, funders, and community stakeholders.

Spatial design

The proposal organizes three designs, each building on the last, across the main building and warehouse. Program zones include the Reuse Store, Tool Library, Donation Intake, Fixer Room, Education Space, Woodshop, Bike Shack, and Makerspace.

Outcome

The final deliverables combine research, floor-plan strategy, physical testing, and cinematic 3D rendering. The resulting models give Seattle REconomy a clear artifact for grant access, partner communication, and future buildout planning as they move toward the new space.
